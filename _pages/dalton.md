# John Dalton

John Dalton, a renowned English chemist and physicist, was born in Cumberland, England in the 18th century, to become one of the most esteemed scientists of his time. As a pedagogue and mentor, Dalton spent much of his life imparting knowledge and skills to the next generation. However, his prodigious interest in meteorology could not be contained, compelling him to conduct extensive research on atmospheric pressure and weather patterns.

In his revolutionary postulation, Dalton proposed the modern atomic theory that has since transformed the understanding of chemistry and physics. He posited that all matter comprises tiny, indivisible particles known as atoms that are immutable and indestructible. Additionally, he propounded that unique substances contain different varieties of atoms, with the ratios of these atoms in a compound dictating its properties.

The profound impact of Dalton's atomic theory cannot be overstated, as it provided the foundation for the modern study of chemistry and physics. His groundbreaking work has forever cemented his place in the annals of history as one of the most important scientists ever.

# So what?

Am I a descendant of John Dalton? Of course not, he never had any children. I am inspired by the man with whom I share a name and a career with. In his time there were no clear distinctions between chemistry and physics, and I have tried to emulate that within my own work today. These different fields are inseparable; although the most funded questions of today tend to focus more on the broader impacts of a phenomenon, I believe it is still a noble pursuit to investigate the fine details which would otherwise be uninteresting to the public.
